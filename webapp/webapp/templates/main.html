About Project Influence Map

In academia, a prevalent question of interest is which academics are influential? Further, what makes an academic influential? With Influence Map, we are working towards a deeper understanding of academic influence by visualising it at a localised level, i.e. considering who a person or a group has been influenced by, and who they have had influence on. We create these visualisations using citation data from the Microsoft Academic Graph.

Methodology

Quantifying influence is difficult for many reasons and it is unlikely that there is a silver bullet solution. Our aim is to use an intuitive yet considered system to quantify localised influence, which we do by summing the number of citations from one person to another, weighted according to the number of authors of the cited paper.

We allow the user to chose whether or not to include self citations in the calculation, where a self-citation occurs if a paper shares any authors with the paper it cites.
